How To Get Over A Breakup

You’ve just been through a painful break up and it feels like your world has collapsed around you. You can’t sleep at night or eat properly. All you want to do is figure out how you going to move on, because now you’re both not together life just isn’t the same! You can’t adjust to the new found single life since everything you did was as a couple. And everything you do just reminds you of them.

They say time heals all open wounds but that’s easier said than done. It is during this time that the wounds are fresh and hurt the most so to ease some of the pain you feel, here are a few tips to guide you on your journey of moving on.

 

Social media is your enemy

Well not Dark Vader sort of enemy but the kind that will only remind you of your ex when you’re still trying to live without them. So block any social interactions you’ve had with your ex, remove them from your Facebook, Twitter, Selfiesnapp or any other social media account. You might be thinking you be cool with having them as friends on Facebook only for them to put up pictures from a night out. The last thing you want is to see your ex posting something that will upset you or remind you of them.

Remove anything that triggers a painful memory

This goes without saying, when trying to process a heartbreak of getting over your ex you want to remove any memento that reminds you of them, this could be a ticket stub that you kept from your first date or little things like a song, smell or place. If you have a keepsake, something given by your ex like a piece of jewellery or an object there is nothing wrong in keeping it. Instead for the time being put it away while you are still going through this emotional state.

Not all relationships end in friendship

So you’re ex wants to be friends and it’s your natural impulse to jump in without thinking. And who can blame you, you’re in a vulnerable place and your minds not thinking straight, so why not let them know you’ll think about it. Even though you want to come across ‘I don’t care’ ‘whatever’ attitude about the breakup, yet your heart is breaking as you try to hold your cool. In all honesty you can never be too sure if you can build a healthy friendship if you haven’t resolved your differences. Sometimes it’s harder to get over someone if you haven’t completely gotten them out of your life but if you feel that you can stay friends best to still break yourself away from any contact that’s including friends/family/social media till you have fully got your life back on track. You don’t have to stop talking to your ex forever just for as long as it takes for you to get over them.

Be careful of rebound relationships

When you get out of one relationship and jump in quickly into another relationship is known as a rebound relationship. When you enter a new relationship with someone quickly soon after your recent breakup, you may be trying to run away from the pain of your old relationship hoping the excitement of the new relationship can somehow camouflage the pain. This isn’t really a wise choice since if the new relationship does not workout you be nursing two breakup at once. So carefully consider if this is the right choice for you and maybe stay single for a while until you fully process your emotion and gotten over your ex.

The urge to drink

If you feel the urge to drink don’t do it alone call some friends. Nothing sadder than being alone and drinking. When going through a break up you might feel the impulse to go out and drink your sorrows away and in the worst case you’ll get so drunk you’ll end up hooking up with someone that you’ll regret later when you sober up! So don’t drink a lone get some friends to keep you company instead, might as well be sad with people who care about.

Drunken text

If drinking and you get tempted to text your ex 1 a.m. get your friends to hide your phone or throw it in a maze! Just because you text them and think that if they reply back that they want you back. This may never be the case and you are only taking a step back instead of moving ahead.

Get out and about

Getting some fresh air will do you some good. Instead of curling under your duvet get yourself out of your emotional cocoon and go for a walk, the fresh crisp air will help clear your head.

Don’t shut out your friends and family

If you have friends or family members who want to give you their support during these hard times, don’t shun them away but embrace the support of others. Having a shoulder to cry on and expressing your feelings to others is a good way to vent and letting everything out. Don’t be afraid to ask for help because having loved ones around their compassionate support is what will remind you again your worthwhile and help you feel good about yourself.

Keep yourself busy

You might feel that you don’t want to do anything or go anywhere. Don’t let that be you, by sitting locked up indoors will only lead to thinking about ‘what went wrong’ or ‘what can you do to change anything’  in the relationship. You want to keep your mind occupied and your days busy. Go out with a few friends who are sure to keep your mind of your ex, check out venues you can attend, or why not take up a new hobby and learn a new skill. Whatever will get you out of the house is what you should be doing. Eventually with all the things happening in your life you’ll soon realize how quickly you’ve forgotten your ex!

Stop putting the blame on yourself or your ex

You need to stop blaming yourself and your ex. No matter how many times you replay ‘who was at fault’ and ‘if only you did something different’ it will never change the fact that you still broke up! You need to stop blaming and start living your life again! Take this time to focus on yourself and recovering to a better you away from these emotional attachments. Think about what it is that you want to change in your life and how you can better your future.

We all know that it hurts like crazy but we also know that you will rise above the pain and be stronger. So don’t feel like this is the end because it is only the beginning to finding your true match.
